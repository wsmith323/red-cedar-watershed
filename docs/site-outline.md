# Proposed Site Outline

## Home

A two-minute explanation of the problem, why it matters, and what the site
contains.

## Why the Lakes Turn Green

Explain phosphorus, cyanobacteria, harmful algae blooms, warm-weather
conditions, reservoir behavior, and internal loading.

## Where the Phosphorus Comes From

Explain the watershed, point sources, nonpoint sources, modeled source
breakdowns, runoff pathways, and the distinction between source categories and
personal blame.

## What Is Already Being Done

Describe monitoring, farm conservation practices, wastewater controls,
wetlands, buffers, dredging, education, research, and coordination.

## What Solutions Are Available

Explain source reduction, treatment wetlands, phosphorus filters, sediment
basins, land protection, nutrient management, and in-lake measures.

## Progress and Remaining Questions

Present available targets, trends, reported accomplishments, data limitations,
and unresolved questions.

## Organizations Involved

Give each organization visible credit and explain its role in plain language.

## Sources and Further Reading

Organize reports and references by subject rather than only by publishing
organization.
