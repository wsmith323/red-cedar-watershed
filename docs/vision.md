# Vision

## Purpose

Create an approachable, evidence-based public resource about Lake Menomin,
Tainter Lake, and the Red Cedar River watershed.

The site should aggregate and explain publicly available information while
recognizing the organizations, researchers, farmers, agencies, and community
groups that have worked on the problem for years.

## Intended audience

- residents of Menomonie and surrounding communities;
- lake users and downstream residents;
- upstream landowners and farmers;
- local officials;
- journalists;
- educators;
- people who know the lakes are impaired but do not understand why.

## Core principles

1. Explain before advocating.
2. Credit existing work.
3. Distinguish evidence from interpretation.
4. Make uncertainty visible.
5. Avoid blame-oriented framing.
6. Show that the problem is difficult without presenting it as inevitable.
7. Treat the website as an explanatory layer over authoritative sources.

## Initial success criteria

A first-time visitor should be able to understand, within several minutes:

- why the lakes turn green;
- why phosphorus matters;
- why the watershed matters;
- what is already being done;
- what kinds of solutions exist;
- where to find authoritative source material.
